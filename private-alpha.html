Elections are dirty.
We’re cleaning them up.

The rise of outside money in American politics has made our campaigns more negative and less honorable.

In too many races, outside groups take over with their dirty money, negative message, and outspend candidate’s small donors by 3 to 1.

This problem exists in all parties, on all sides of a given issue, and that is why it needs a dedicated non-partisan effort to clean it up.

A solution that has worked.

The 2012 Massachusetts senate race used a contract between the candidates called “The People’s Pledge” to exclude outside money.

The candidates agreed that if any outside group spent on their behalf that they would give to charity out of their own funds.

It worked. Outside spending was drastically reduced to merely 9% of total spending in contrast to upwards of 60% in other states.

Who we are

Simply a group of concerned citizens who see a need for change in our political system, and aren’t willing to wait for legislative action to fix the escalating problem of money.

Jim Greer, Co-Founder

Jim Greer is the founder and CEO of Kongregate, a web and mobile platform that distributes over 60,000 games. Kongregate reaches over 15 million unique visitors, and was acquired by GameStop in 2010. He has worked in the game industry since 1991 and holds a Computer Science degree from Princeton.

Zack Booth Simpson, Co-Founder

Zack Booth Simpson is a Research Fellow at the University of Texas Center for Systems and Synthetic Biology. He has published papers in Nature, Cell, and other journals. He is also an interactive artist who has exhibited at dozens of museums, including Chicago’s Museum of Science and Industry and New York’s Hall of Science. He is self-educated.

Mark McKinnon, Campaign Guru

Mark McKinnon has solved complex strategic challenges for causes, companies and candidates, including President George W. Bush, Senator John McCain, Governor Ann Richards, Congressman "Good Time" Charlie Wilson and Bono. He has helped engineer five winning presidential primary and general elections. He is co-founder of No Labels, which seeks to promote a new politics of problem solving over point-scoring.

Trevor Potter, Legal Counsel

Trevor Potter is an attorney at Caplin & Drysdale in Washington, D.C. and leads the firm's Political Law Practice. He is a former Chairman of the Federal Election Commission and served as General Counsel to John McCain’s 2008 and 2000 presidential campaigns. He now represents Stephen Colbert and his SuperPAC and advises him on campaign finance issues on and off the air.

Matt Cutts, Founding Donor

Matt Cutts is head of Google’s Webspam team, and a major public face for explaning how search works to webmasters. He wrote the first version of SafeSearch, Google’s family friendly filter.

Jon Hutchens, Political Advisor

Jon Hutchens is president of Media Strategies & Research which is one of the largest political media placement and strategy firms in the country. Jon has been involved in elections throughout the country helping to elect candidates for president, U.S. Senate and Congress, governors and mayors.

Kahlil Byrd, Political Advisor

Kahlil Byrd, is a political entrepreneur. Currently, he serves as Cheif Investment Officer for the Fund for the Republic. As former CEO of Americans Elect and president of StudentsFirst, Kahlil has extensive experience building and guiding complex national organizations, gathering resources to power innovative reform ideas, and operating in national politics.
